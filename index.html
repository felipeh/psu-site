Felipe Hernández

Assistant Professor, Penn State University

Google Scholar

I am mathematical physicist interested in understanding the emergence of large-scale physics (which is diffusive, dissipative, and classical) from microscopic physics (which is time-reversible and classical). I am also interested in computational aspects of quantum mechanics (when is it easy to simulate physics, and when is it hard?)

I completed my PhD in 2023 at Stanford University, advised by Lenya Ryzhik. In 2023-2024 I was a CLE Moore Instructor and NSF Postdoctoral Fellow at MIT.
